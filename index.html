Charitable Organisations

Save the Children

Save the children was founded in England in 1919 by Eglantyne Jebb. Since then they have expanded into 30 other countries around the world. Save the Children provide funds and assistance to improve children's health, education, and wellbeing, and to assist in emergencies.

Berry Street

Berry Street was originally founded in 1877 “to give shelter to the unfortunate, the dying babies and the fallen women”. They work with children and families to help recover from childhood abuse and trauma.

CanTeen

CanTeen helps young people deal with the challenges of cancer. They provide counselling, peer supprot programs, and information services about cancer, whether it affects you, or someone you know.

UNICEF

UNICEF stands for “United Nations Children's Fund”, and works to uphold the rights of children based on the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child. They educate children in the concept of rights and how the rights of other children can be respected.
